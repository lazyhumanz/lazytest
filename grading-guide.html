Error Parameters - Detailed Guidelines

Critical Fail Errors (50 points - Auto-Fail)

Description: Error that directly results in customer leaving or cancelling service.

When to Apply:

  • Customer explicitly states they are cancelling due to the agent's error
  • Customer threatens to leave and follows through
  • Agent provides information that causes customer to cancel subscription/service
  • Critical data breach or privacy violation that leads to customer churn

Examples:

  • Agent incorrectly tells customer their account will be charged incorrectly, leading to cancellation
  • Agent accidentally deletes customer data that results in customer leaving
  • Agent is rude/disrespectful to a point where customer cancels

Important Notes:

This is an auto-fail error. If this error occurs, the audit fails immediately regardless of other scores.

Description: Violation of zero tolerance policy or major compliance breach.

When to Apply:

  • Violation of company's zero tolerance policies (check your organization's specific policies)
  • Major compliance violations (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, etc.)
  • Discriminatory language or behavior
  • Sharing confidential customer information inappropriately
  • Fraudulent activity or intentional misrepresentation

Examples:

  • Agent shares customer payment information with unauthorized party
  • Agent uses discriminatory language based on race, gender, religion, etc.
  • Agent intentionally misleads customer for personal gain
  • Violation of regulatory requirements that could result in legal action

Important Notes:

This is an auto-fail error. Review your organization's zero tolerance policy document for specific violations.

Critical Errors (10 points)

Description: Providing wrong information to customer that could cause confusion or issues.

When to Apply:

  • Agent provides factually incorrect information about products/services
  • Agent gives wrong pricing, dates, or deadlines
  • Agent misinforms customer about policies or procedures
  • Information provided could lead to customer taking incorrect action

Examples:

  • Agent says product costs $99 when it actually costs $149
  • Agent tells customer refund will process in 3 days when it takes 7-10 days
  • Agent provides incorrect account information

Count Multiple Instances:

If agent provides multiple incorrect pieces of information, count each instance separately.

Description: Unprofessional behavior, language, or tone during interaction.

When to Apply:

  • Using inappropriate language (profanity, slang, overly casual)
  • Sarcastic, condescending, or dismissive tone
  • Interrupting customer repeatedly
  • Making personal comments unrelated to the issue
  • Arguing with customer instead of de-escalating

Examples:

  • "Well, you should have read the terms..." (condescending)
  • Using profanity or inappropriate language
  • Rolling eyes or making dismissive gestures (in video calls)
  • Talking over the customer

Description: Failed to escalate when needed, or escalated unnecessarily.

When to Apply - Lack of Escalation:

  • Customer requests supervisor/manager and agent refuses
  • Issue clearly requires higher authority (refunds over limit, policy exceptions)
  • Customer is extremely upset and escalation protocol requires it
  • Technical issue beyond agent's scope

When to Apply - Unnecessary Escalation:

  • Agent escalates simple issues they could handle themselves
  • Escalating without attempting to resolve first
  • Escalating issues that are clearly within agent's authority

Description: Insufficient investigation into customer issue or concern.

When to Apply:

  • Agent makes assumptions without checking account details
  • Doesn't review customer history before responding
  • Gives generic response without understanding specific issue
  • Doesn't ask clarifying questions when issue is unclear
  • Fails to check multiple systems/tools that might contain relevant information

Examples:

  • Customer says "I was charged twice" - agent doesn't check transaction history
  • Agent provides solution without understanding the root cause
  • Agent doesn't review notes from previous interactions

Significant Errors (10 points)

Description: Not showing appropriate understanding or concern for customer situation.

When to Apply:

  • Customer expresses frustration/upset and agent responds mechanically
  • No acknowledgment of customer's emotional state
  • Using scripted responses without personalization
  • Minimizing customer's concerns
  • Not apologizing when company/agent error is evident

Examples:

  • Customer: "I've been waiting 3 hours!" Agent: "I understand. How can I help?" (too mechanical)
  • Customer: "This ruined my day" - Agent doesn't acknowledge the impact
  • Agent says "I understand" but tone and words don't show genuine concern

Description: Lacking necessary product or service knowledge to assist customer.

When to Apply:

  • Agent doesn't know basic product/service features
  • Unable to answer common questions about offerings
  • Doesn't use available resources (knowledge base, documentation) to find answers
  • Provides "I don't know" without offering to find out

Note:

This is different from "Incorrect Information" - here the agent admits they don't know, while "Incorrect Information" means they gave wrong information.

Description: Not properly communicating or following up internally as required.

When to Apply:

  • Fails to document interaction in required systems
  • Doesn't follow up with other departments as promised
  • Doesn't create internal tickets when required
  • Fails to notify supervisor/team about important issues
  • Doesn't update customer records with relevant information

Major Errors (5 points)

Description: Important information missing from interaction or documentation.

When to Apply:

  • Agent doesn't collect required customer information
  • Missing details in case notes that would be needed for follow-up
  • Doesn't confirm important details (account number, order ID, etc.)
  • Fails to document resolution steps taken

Description: Using outdated templates, macros, or scripts.

When to Apply:

  • Using templates that reference old policies or procedures
  • Macros containing outdated information
  • Using retired scripts or processes

Description: Providing too much information at once, overwhelming the customer.

When to Apply:

  • Agent provides lengthy explanations when simple answer would suffice
  • Dumps too much technical information on customer
  • Provides multiple solutions without allowing customer to process
  • Information delivery is confusing or hard to follow

Description: Spelling or grammar mistakes in written communication.

When to Apply:

  • Spelling mistakes in emails, chats, or documentation
  • Grammar errors that affect clarity
  • Poor sentence structure
  • Multiple errors in a single message

Count Multiple Instances:

Count each instance separately. For example, if an email has 3 spelling mistakes, that's 3 instances of this error.

Minor Errors (2.5 points)

Description: Too little or too much customer engagement during interaction.

Inadequate Engagement:

  • Too brief responses without elaboration
  • Not asking follow-up questions when needed
  • Being too quiet or unresponsive

Excessive Engagement:

  • Over-sharing personal information
  • Too chatty when customer wants quick resolution
  • Asking too many unnecessary questions

Description: Minor information gaps that do not significantly impact service delivery.

When to Apply:

  • Minor details missing that don't affect resolution
  • Small gaps in documentation that can be easily inferred
  • Optional information not collected

Note:

This is for minor gaps. For significant missing information, use "Missing Information" (5 points) instead.

Scoring Basics

The calculation depends on the scorecard type:

Deductive:

Final Score = 100 - (Sum of all error point deductions)

Example: 100 - (5 + 5 + 10) = 80%

Additive:

Final Score = Sum of all achievement points

Example: 10 + 10 + 5 = 25%

Hybrid:

Final Score = Starting Score - Errors + Achievements + Bonuses

Example: 100 - 5 + 10 + 10 = 115% (if over-100 allowed)

The passing threshold is set per scorecard. An audit must meet or exceed this threshold to pass.

Auto-Fail Rules:

Even if the final score is above the threshold, any "Critical Fail Error" (50 points, auto-fail flag) will cause the audit to fail automatically.

Counter Field (for Errors):

  • Used for errors that can occur multiple times
  • You enter the number of times the error occurred
  • Points are multiplied by the count
  • Example: 3 instances of "Grammatical Errors" (5 points each) = -15 points total

Radio Field (for Achievements):

  • Used for achievements that are binary (Yes/No)
  • Either the achievement was demonstrated or it wasn't
  • Points are awarded once if "Yes" is selected
  • Example: "Excellent Empathy" = Yes = +10 points (one time only)

Common Scenarios

Use the most severe category. For example, if an agent provides incorrect information that leads to a loss of business, use "Error Leading to a Loss of Business" (50 points) rather than "Incorrect Information" (10 points).

General Rule:

Always apply the highest severity error that fits the situation. Do not double-penalize by applying multiple error categories for the same mistake.

When in doubt, consider the impact on the customer experience and the likelihood of negative consequences.

Guidelines:

  • Consider customer impact: Did this affect their ability to resolve their issue?
  • Consider severity: Could this lead to complaints or escalations?
  • When uncertain between two categories, choose the lower severity
  • Document your reasoning in the feedback section

If the error is caught and corrected during the same interaction, you may reduce the penalty or not apply it, depending on the severity and whether the customer noticed.

Recommendation:

  • If self-corrected before customer impact: Consider reducing penalty by 50% or not applying it
  • If customer noticed but agent corrected: Apply full penalty but note the correction in feedback
  • Critical Fail Errors: Even if corrected, these should still be marked due to their severity
